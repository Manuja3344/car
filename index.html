GTR 35 EVOLUTION 

the GTR35 evolution refers to the history and development of the Nissan GT-R (R35), a sports car and grand tourer produced by Nissan since 2007. It is the successor to the Nissan Skyline GT-R, a high-performance variant of the Nissan Skyline. The GT-R (R35) is built on the PM platform, which is derived from the FM platform used in the Skyline and the Nissan Z models1 The GT-R (R35) has had several facelifts, updates and special editions throughout the years. The first generation was launched at the 2007 Tokyo Motor Show and featured a 3.8 L twin-turbocharged VR38DETT V6 engine with 480 hp. The second generation was introduced in 2010 with minor cosmetic changes and a power increase to 530 hp. The third generation was unveiled in 2016 with a new interior and a power boost to 565 hp. The fourth generation was revealed in 2023 with a refined exterior and a power output of 570 hp2 The GT-R (R35) has also spawned various sub-models, such as the Nismo, the Track Edition, the SpecV, the Black Edition, the Egoist, the Pure Edition, the Premium Edition and the GT-R50 by Italdesign. Each of these models has its own unique features and specifications that cater to different tastes and preferences of customers

Lamborghini Aventador  EVOLUTION  

the Lamborghini Aventador evolution refers to the history and development of the Lamborghini Aventador, a sports car and grand tourer produced by Lamborghini since 2011. It is the successor to the Lamborghini Murciélago, a high-performance variant of the Lamborghini Diablo. The Aventador is built on a carbon-fiber monocoque chassis and features a 6.5 L naturally aspirated V12 engine with various power outputs depending on the model1 The Aventador has had several facelifts, updates and special editions throughout the years. The first generation was launched at the 2011 Geneva Motor Show and featured a 6.5 L V12 engine with 700 hp. The second generation was introduced in 2015 as the SuperVeloce (SV) with a power increase to 750 hp and a weight reduction of 50 kg. The third generation was unveiled in 2016 as the Aventador S with a power boost to 740 hp and a new four-wheel steering system. The fourth generation was revealed in 2018 as the Aventador SVJ (SuperVeloce Jota) with a power output of 770 hp and improved aerodynamics2 The Aventador has also spawned various sub-models, such as the Roadster, the Anniversario, the Miura Homage, the Pirelli Edition, the Veneno, the Centenario and the Sian. Each of these models has its own unique features and specifications that cater to different tastes and preferences of customers

 Toyota Supra MK4  EVOLUTION

the Toyota Supra MK4 evolution refers to the history and development of the Toyota Supra MK4, a sports car and grand tourer produced by Toyota from 1993 to 2002. It is the successor to the Toyota Supra MK3, a high-performance variant of the Toyota Celica. The Supra MK4 is built on a rear-wheel-drive platform and features a 3.0 L inline-6 engine with either a naturally aspirated or a twin-turbocharged version1 The Supra MK4 has had several facelifts, updates and special editions throughout the years. The first generation was launched in 1993 and featured a 3.0 L 2JZ-GE engine with 220 hp or a 3.0 L 2JZ-GTE engine with 276 hp. The second generation was introduced in 1996 with minor cosmetic changes and a power increase to 225 hp for the naturally aspirated engine and 320 hp for the twin-turbocharged engine. The third generation was unveiled in 1997 with a new front bumper, headlights, taillights and spoiler. The fourth generation was revealed in 1998 with a new six-speed manual transmission for the twin-turbocharged engine and a new TRD body kit2 The Supra MK4 has also spawned various sub-models, such as the SZ, RZ, SZ-R, RZ-S, Aerotop and Anniversary Edition. Each of these models has its own unique features and specifications that cater to different tastes and preferences of customers
